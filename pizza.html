Recipe adapted from this recipe, which itself is adapted from the Ken Forkish recipe in Flour, Water, Salt. Note: this makes 2 servings of pizza dough. Ingredients:
  • 500g AP flour
  • 375g warm water, divided
  • 1g active dry yeast
  • 10g fine salt
Steps:
  1. Mix flour and all but 1 tbsp of the water in a large bowl and set it aside for 20 minutes. Mix the remaining 1 tbsp water with the yeast -- Don't expect the yeast to bubble up like it does when making milk bread, it will just seem to dissolve and get cloudy. Also the specific temperature of the warm water is not super important here.
  2. Sprinkle salt over the surface of the dough. Stir yeast mixture and pour it over the dough. Fold the dough: reach under the dough and pull a section over the top. Repeat until the center of the dough is covered. Then, using your thumb and forefinger as pincers, squeeze and pinch 5-6 chunks of dough (it will look kind of like the michelin man's arm). Repeat the folding and pinching until the yeast and salt are fully incorporated and a shaggy dough is formed. Cover the bowl and let rest for about an hour.
  3. Reach underneath the dough and pull a section over the top. Repeat about 5 times while rotating the bowl, until the dough has tightened up into a ball. Take the dough and put it into a new, lightly greased bowl. Cover and let rise until doubled in volume, about 5- 6 hours.
  4. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface, and divide it into two portions. Working with one piece at a time, stretch out a quarter of the dough just until you feel resistance, then fold it back over the center. Repeat with the other 3 quarters of dough until the center is covered and a loose ball is formed. Turn the ball over seam side down and place in an un-floured area. Cup your hands around the dough and gently pull it towards you, dragging the bottom of the dough across the counter to create tension. Rotate the ball 90 degrees and repeat. Keep repeating until the top of the dough tightens up and the ball is round.
  5. Transfer to a greased plate and repeat with the other portion of dough. Lightly oil the tops, cover, and let rest at room temperature for about an hour. Preheat oven at this time. If not using right away, cover tightly with plastic wrap and refrigerate until ready (for up to 2 days).
  6. Preheat the oven to 500F. Flour a baking pan and set aside.
  7. Pick up a piece of dough and quickly rotate it like a steering wheel while gravity pulls it down into a 12-inch pizza shape. (Note: I do this part really fast because letting it stretch too much makes the pizza too thin in the middle. I do this part just to get a general circle shape before shaping and stretching it further on the pan.)
    8. For a caccio e pepe pizza:
  8. Microwave 2 minced garlic cloves with 1 tbsp of butter, then brush over the center of the pizza, leaving the 1-inch perimeter empty. Brush olive oil over the crust. Add 4 tbsp of mozzarella and less than 1/3 cup whole milk ricotta cheese (1/3 cup is too much) on top. Bake for 15 minutes, then remove from the oven and add anchovies and arugula. Grate parmesan cheese over the top, and generously season with black pepper. Then broil the pizza on high for 1 minute.
    9. For prosciutto pizza:
  9. Top the pizza with pizza sauce and 4 tbsp mozzarella, leaving the 1-inch perimeter empty. Brush olive oil over the crust. Bake for 15 minutes, then remove from the oven. Top with arugula and prosciutto, then grate parmesan cheese over the top. Then broil the pizza on high for 1 minute.
